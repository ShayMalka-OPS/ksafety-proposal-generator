# K-Safety Proposal Generator — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Kabatone's internal sales team. Used on laptops, tablets, and mobile phones — in the office, in transit, and occasionally in client-facing settings. The primary workflow is generating a fresh proposal from scratch after a call or meeting with a city client, then exporting it to PDF or Word for delivery. Speed matters, but the output must look credible enough to hand to a government procurement officer or city director.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative. Precise. Measured.**

Not startup-bubbly, not militaristic, not cold. Think of the visual register of a serious infrastructure company — the kind that sells to city governments. Institutional gravity with modern clarity. The interface should feel like it was designed by engineers who respect the customer's time. No flair for its own sake.

### Brand Palette (fixed — do not deviate)
- Dark Blue: `#1A3A5C`
- Mid Blue: `#1E6BA8`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Gold (#F0A500) has been retired — do not use it.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Mid-light theme.** Not pure white — a cool, slightly blue-tinted off-white background (tinted toward the Dark Blue hue). Dark Blue dominates the navigation and structural chrome. Mid Blue for interactive elements and CTAs. White for content surfaces.

**Reference feel**: Salesforce.com's structural clarity and enterprise seriousness — without copying its visual language. Think: a well-designed government infrastructure procurement platform. Dense enough to convey capability, spacious enough to breathe.

**Anti-references**:
- No startup gradient heroes (the current dark-blue-to-mid-blue linear-gradient hero must go)
- No emoji icons in feature lists or cards
- No identical 3-col card grids (icon + heading + 2 lines text repeated 6 times)
- No pure-white-everything + shadow-sm cards
- Nothing that looks like a Tailwind UI or shadcn/ui template out of the box

### Typography Direction
**Display / Headings**: Barlow Condensed — tight, governmental, reads like text on a city hall nameplate or a NATO infrastructure spec. Bold at large sizes, authoritative without being decorative. Available on Google Fonts.

**Body / UI**: Hanken Grotesk — precise Swiss-style grotesque, readable at 14px on mobile, slightly warmer than a pure neutral sans. Available on Google Fonts.

Use fixed `rem` scales for the UI (this is an app tool, not a marketing site). Minimum 5-step type scale with at least 1.25× ratio between steps.

### Design Principles
1. **Structure over decoration.** Every visual element must earn its place. No decorative gradients, no emoji, no rounded shadow-cards that exist only to look "designed."
2. **Tables are first-class citizens.** Pricing tables, infrastructure requirements, and proposal summaries are the core output. They must be the most refined element on any page.
3. **Left-aligned authority.** Centered layouts read as a landing page. Left-aligned layouts with deliberate asymmetry read as a product built for professionals.
4. **Responsive without compromise.** Sales reps use phones and tablets. Every interaction must work at 375px — wizard steps, pricing toggles, export buttons — without hiding critical functionality.
5. **Restraint on color.** Dark Blue and Mid Blue are structural. White is for content surfaces. Color should signal action or state, never be used for decoration.
